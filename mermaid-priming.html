Middle English negation

The change in negation

  • In Middle English, there is a change in the exponence of Neg
  • The negator ne, inherited from OE, is lost
  • not, formerly a negative adverb, becomes the new negator

Details of the change

  • During the period of the change, a large number of negative sentences have both ne and not

    he ne shal nou ít decieue him (Early Prose Psalter, 161:131:11, from Frisch 1997)

Negation in ME

Two analyses

  • Frisch (1997): there are only two mental objects, ne and not
    • Sentences with both are the result of both being activated at once
  • Wallage (2008, 2012): ne + not is its own mental object with discernably different properties from either of the individual ingredients

Priming analysis

  • Frisch predicts that sentences with ne…not should facilitate ne just as much as sentences with ne alone

  • False

Priming analysis

  • Wallage predicts that each of the three types should facilitate only itself

  • Basically true (with some extra interpretation)

Conclusions

  • The priming data supports Wallage ºs account more than Frisch ºs
  • It forms one piece of the evidence to reject Frisch ºs account

Do support

Background

  • In Early Modern English, do support is innovated in NICE contexts
    • negation, inversion, contrast, ellipsis
  • Additionally, auxiliary do is inserted in affirmative declaratives quite often
    • Peak of 10%
    • Important: not do support

Do as argument structure marker

  • I ºve argued that before 1600, do functions as a marker of agentive subjectscite
    • Explains its appearance in affirmative declaratives, among other facts
  • This is true even in NICE contexts
  • My claim: there is no (or very little) do-support in English before 1600
  • After 1600, NI(CE) contexts have do-support whereas affirmative declaratives maintain agent-marker do (archaism)

Priming consequences

  • Prediction: pre-1600 facilitation between affirmative declarative do and NICE do
    • Because they are the same mental object
  • Post-1600 no facilitation

    • They are no longer the same

Modeling priming vs. style-shifting

Style shifting

  • Style shifting could create effects that look like priming
    • In the extreme, a sequence of variants AAAA…BBBB…
  • More of a concern in naturalistic settings than experimentally
  • But, we might be able to use statistical modeling to control for this

GA(M)Ms

  • Traditional regression models: the mathematical form of a time-varying component is given
    • Linear, cubic, …
  • Generalized Additive Model: fit as much (or as little) time-varying structure as needed
  • G. A. Mixed Model: same relationship to GAM as lm ~ lmm
